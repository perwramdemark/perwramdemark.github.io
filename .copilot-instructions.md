# Blog Post Writing Guidelines

## Em-Dash Usage

**Never use the em-dash character (—) in blog posts.**

Instead of using em-dashes, rephrase sentences to avoid needing dashes. This improves clarity and readability.

### Examples

❌ **Don't do this:**
- "Vue Router, Pinia, and other tools have evolved significantly — here's what's new."
- "The conference was intense — great speakers and insights."

✅ **Do this instead:**
- "Vue Router, Pinia, and other tools have evolved significantly. Here's what's new."
- "The conference was intense with great speakers and valuable insights."

### Strategy

When you encounter a need for an em-dash:
1. **Split into separate sentences** for dramatic pauses or emphasis
2. **Use connecting words** like "because," "since," "as," "that," "which"
3. **Restructure the sentence** to be more direct
4. **Remove unnecessary clauses** that would require a dash

This applies to all blog post creation and editing in this repository.
